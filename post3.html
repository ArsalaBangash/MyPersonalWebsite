Dev Journal May 9th, 2017

Decisions

I can’t start working on the app yet. I still have so much to learn about iOS and Android application development, and jumping into development at this moment wouldn’t be the most prudent option. I have 13 units of iOS education left and 12 units of Android education left. At 1 unit from each course a day, I would have a confident grasp of iOS and Android knowledge by May 22nd.

I need an engine. The engine will drive the entire app. This isn’t an app that’s going to have a user evaluate a simple binary or unary operation. There needs to be a core mathematical engine that evaluates expressions such as log(100) + 2(36) - 8. Only through intense mathematical rigour such as this can the app truly affect a user’s mathematical ability.

There are two key components to the engine: One component has to generate a mathematical expression, and the other component has to take that expression and display it to the user in a visually appealing manner.

To generate a mathematical expression, I plan on constructing an expression tree. Depending on the current mode and the relative restrictions, the tree would be generated accordingly. Alright that was too vague...let me rephrase. If the user is on the medium mode and has chosen the operators +, -, and *, then the graph generation algorithm will generate a binary or ternary operation because of the medium difficulty, and it will place the given operators as the relative root nodes to evaluate the respective children operands.

Displaying these generated expressions is a different beast altogether. From what I can see, the support for mathematical equation rendering on both platforms is absolute garbage, and there are third party libraries to support Latex math rendering on both iOS and Android via WebViews. I have found two that could work to my specifications, and both operate under the MIT license. Under my current architecture, every question type needed to have a presentQuestion() method, and under the new architecture, instead of returning a string, the method will have to walk the entire expression tree, format it into an acceptable latex string, which will then be displayed.
